Privacy & Technical Trade-offs

NEAR: The Cryptographic Vault

NEAR approaches privacy via Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) and MPC. The goal is to run the model where no one (not even the node runner) sees the weights or data.

High Computational Privacy

Telegram: The Secure Tunnel

TON leverages standard encryption and Edge Computing. By running logic on the user's device (inside the Mini App) or standard servers, they prioritize speed. Privacy is focused on Anonymity of the user (via TON DNS/Numbers) rather than hiding the computation itself.

Medium Computational Privacy

Unique Technical Solutions

NEAR PROTOCOL

Nightshade Sharding

Allows linear scaling. As more AI agents join the network, the network adds more shards. Crucial for high-throughput agent communication.

TELEGRAM / TON

Infinite Sharding

TON also uses dynamic sharding, but optimized for message-passing concurrency, mimicking the actor model of Erlang. Perfect for millions of small, fast interactions.

NEAR PROTOCOL

FastAuth

Biometric login (FaceID/TouchID) that recovers keys. Makes using an AI agent as simple as unlocking your phone.

TELEGRAM / TON

TON Proxy & Sites

Decentralized VPN/Proxy layer built into the chain. AI Agents can host decentralized frontends accessible via .ton domains inside Telegram.

The Verdict

The market will likely bifurcate. Telegram will capture the Consumer AI market (chatbots, entertainment, simple assistants) due to friction-less UX.

NEAR is positioned to capture the Agentic AI market (autonomous finance, complex workflows, cross-chain execution) due to its superior technical abstraction and chain signatures.
